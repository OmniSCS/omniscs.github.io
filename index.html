OmniSCS: Omni Safety-Critical Scenario Synthesis for Autonomous Driving via a Fully Editable Driving World

arXiv Code

Abstract

Generating high-fidelity safety-critical scenarios (SCS) is essential for ensuring the safety and robustness of autonomous driving systems. Recent studies have explored reconstructing high-quality 3D scenes from recorded driving logs using Neural Radiance Fields and Gaussian Splatting. However, these methods primarily focus on replaying original scenes and are not specifically designed to integrate new objects for creating SCS. Moreover, directly modifying agent trajectories to synthesize SCS often leads to noticeable artifacts in sensor data, due to the sparsity of input views in driving logs. Hence, in this paper, we present OmniSCS, a high-fidelity safety-critical scenario synthesis framework based on real-world driving logs. The framework consists of two key modules: 1) the Fully Editable Driving World Construction module is designed to maintain the high-fidelity of agent appearance and background during scene editing. Specifically, we propose a dual-strategy agent reconstruction method to achieve 360° editing of agents under diverse visual conditions. And we propose a Depth-refinement Background Reconstruction method to address the background degradation challenges posed by moving static agents. 2) the Scenario Synthesis module facilitates the insertion of objects into suitable locations and editing agents’ trajectories to generate diverse SCS. Experimental results demonstrate that OmniSCS achieves superior data fidelity in edited scenes compared to existing state-of-the-art methods. Furthermore, OmniSCS enables both the improvement of autonomous driving algorithm performance and the implementation of closed-loop evaluation. These results indicate that OmniSCS provides a safer and more effective approach for optimizing and testing SCS in autonomous driving.

Method Overview

Overview of OmniSCS. (a) The Fulling Editable Driving World Construction (EDW) module generates a vehicle dataset pool for 3D agent reconstruction under varying conditions and creating the scenario background using absolute depth information (b) The Scenario Synthesis module facilitates the insertion of appearance-level objects into suitable locations and editing agents’ trajectories to generate behavior-level risky maneuvers. This enables 1-to-N SCS synthesis, allowing the generation of diverse scenarios from a single original scene, thereby enhancing AVs performance and supporting closed-loop testing in dynamic environments through real-time sensor feedback.
